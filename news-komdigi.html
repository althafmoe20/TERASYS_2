Komdigi Reveals Reasons for Suspending TikTok's

October 4, 2025 TERASYS Newsroom

The Ministry of Communication and Information (Komdigi) has taken firm action by temporarily freezing the Electronic System Operator (PSE) license of TikTok in Indonesia. This decision was made after a thorough evaluation of compliance with local regulations.

Komdigi stated that the suspension aims to ensure that all digital platforms operating in Indonesia adhere to the country's data protection and content moderation laws. The ministry emphasized the importance of safeguarding user data and maintaining a safe digital environment.

"We are committed to protecting Indonesian users and ensuring that all platforms comply with our regulations," said a Komdigi spokesperson.

The ministry is currently in discussions with TikTok to resolve the issues and restore the platform's operations as soon as possible.
